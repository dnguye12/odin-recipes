ODIN RECIPES

Sides

Description

Piping hot and perfectly salted.

Ingredients

  • Russet potatoes
  • Oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Start with cleaned and peeled russet potatoes that you have cut into 3/8-inch "match sticks."
  2. Place in bowl of very cold water: keep rinsing and changing the water until the water is clear; drain thoroughly and dry with paper towels or a clean lint-free kitchen towel.
  3. Meanwhile, you preheat your hot oil to 350 degrees F.
  4. Place prepared taters in oil and cook about 5 minutes. They will have that blond-tone color to them.
  5. Note: Once you add cold potatoes to the hot oil, the temperature of your oil is going to drop - you want it to be somewhere between 330 - 325 degrees F.
  6. Remove from oil; drain and cool.
  7. Now - either refrigerate until ready to finish cooking, or cool completely and freeze up to 3 months. To freeze properly - place completely cooled fries in single layer on tray and place in freezer until frozen. Then bag them.
  8. To finish cooking - preheat your oil to 400* F. Add your cold fries (which will drop the oil temp - which is fine because you want it near the 375 degrees F. temp) and cook a few minutes until done.
  9. Lightly salt them and shake well so that the salt distributes well and they are not salty.
